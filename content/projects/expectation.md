As we listen to music, our brain constantly generates expectations for what might happen next.
These expectations come from the structure of the music itself, and the way that we interpret it
in the context of our lifetime history of music listening.
As the music continues, the expectations can variously be satisfied, frustrated, or delayed;
these dynamics of expectation have a strong emotional effect on us, and contribute integrally
to the music's interest and aesthetic effect.
